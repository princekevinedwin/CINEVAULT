Easter Eggs & Hidden References

Pixar's "A113"

In almost every Pixar film, you can find the code "A113" somewhere in the background. This is the classroom number where many Pixar animators learned their craft. It appears in movies like Toy Story, Finding Nemo, and The Incredibles.

Stan Lee Cameos

Before his passing, Stan Lee made cameo appearances in nearly every Marvel movie. His most memorable cameos include playing a hot dog vendor in "X-Men" and a strip club DJ in "Deadpool."

The Tarantino Universe

Many of Quentin Tarantino's films exist in the same universe. Characters from one film often appear in another, and brands like "Red Apple" cigarettes appear across multiple movies.

Behind-the-Scenes

The Making of Avatar

James Cameron waited over a decade to make "Avatar" because he felt technology wasn't advanced enough to realize his vision. The film pioneered new motion capture techniques.

Practical Effects in Mad Max

"Mad Max: Fury Road" used minimal CGI, with over 80% of the effects being practical. The team built 150 custom vehicles for the film's chase sequences.

Movie Firsts & Records

First Color Film

"The Wizard of Oz" (1939) was one of the first feature-length films to be shot in Technicolor, revolutionizing the use of color in cinema.

Highest-Grossing Film

"Avatar" (2009) remains the highest-grossing film of all time with over $2.9 billion worldwide, a record it has held for over a decade.

Longest Film Ever Made

"The Cure for Insomnia" (1987) runs for 87 hours, consisting primarily of artist L.D. Groban reading his 4,000-page poem "A Cure for Insomnia."
